Mercury is the smallest and closest planet to the Sun in our solar system.
Mercury has a longer day than year. It takes 176 Earth days to rotate once, but only 88 Earth days to orbit the Sun

Venus is the second planet from the sun and the second brightest object in the night sky.
Venus is named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the largest of the terrestrial planets.
Earth is the only planet in our solar system not to be named after a Greek or Roman deity

Mars is sometimes called the Red Planet because of rusty iron in the ground.
Mars is half the size of Earth and has a very thin atmosphere made of carbon dioxide, nitrogen, and argon

Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system and is the fifth planet out from the Sun.
It is two and a half times more massive than all the other planets in the solar system combined.

Saturn is the second-largest and the flattest planet in the Solar System.
Saturn is the most distant planet that can be seen with the naked eye from Earth.
